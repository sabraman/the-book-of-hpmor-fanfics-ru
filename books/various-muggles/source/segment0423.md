Harry is required to point his wand at the ground, and is doing so. He has been given 60 seconds in which it would be a violation of a statement/promise made in Parseltongue for Voldemort to kill him, if he is just standing there and not raising his wand or the like.

To further guarantee that sixty seconds, an appropriate first step may be to truthfully say in Parseltongue, before either further developing any plan or coming up with a useful secret to tell Voldemort, "I need to think".

Harry's second step should be to inhale and more or less hold his breath, in a non-obvious fashion, in effect pretending to be hyperventilating and needing to calm himself down.

The third and first key step should be to Transfigure a curved cylindrical pathway in the ground, ending under Harry's feet, although the portion under his feet should be the last to be Transfigured. The very edge of the cylinder should be Transfigured into a tough, slick liner such as smooth plastic. The portion just inside that edge should be Transfigured into a light, non-toxic lubricant such as vegetable oil. The rest should be Transfigured into air, which Harry will be careful not to breathe. The curved path should have the shape of a moderately shallow partial parabola with the minimum/apex under and offset from Harry, which rather than curving all the way back up to the surface ends in a short horizontal ledge shortly after the minimum, at a height such that falling to the bottom and curving back up to the ledge, in the presence of the liner and lubricant, will not injure Harry, or at least will only leave him with some bruises rather than any open wounds or injuries bad enough to compel him to breathe before he has come to the end of the first cylinder.

When this first Transfiguration is complete, Harry should therefore fall suddenly below the surface and be propelled, by gravity, some distance away to the ending ledge underground. Neither Voldemort nor any of the Death Eaters will be able to fire a curse at him in a straight line, and if Voldemort sprays acid down into the tunnel, it will not reach Harry until he is able to fill the entire bottom of the parabola and reach the height of the ledge. But by then, Harry will have moved on to step four.

In step four, after coming to rest on the horizontal ledge, Harry should Transmogrify another shallowly sloping pathway, roughly the mirror-image of the first one, but with certain key differences. First, this pathway will be designed to climb up out of, not slide down through. The edge will be an airtight but non-slippery material, such as tacky rubber or even pencil-eraser material. Second, the center portion should be not air, but a brightly colored, nontoxic, nonirritating liquid relatively easy to swim through and with very limited evaporation, preferably a weak basic solution- nothing caustic, but basically baking soda in light oil with red food coloring, to help neutralize any acid that may be sprayed into the tunnel after Harry and enable him to keep track of the fluid later. The pathway should end at a point within eyeshot and Accio range of the pile containing Harry's clothes, pouch and Time-Turner, but \_not\_ right next to them, because Voldemort will be expecting him to turn up there.

Hermione is some distance from the tunnel entrance and should not be affected if these steps are completed quickly, plus has increased resistance to damage if she does somehow choke on a bit of earth.

At this point, the reappearing earth may push the slightly basic liquid back towards Harry or may simply be saturated with some of it. Harry realizes the most likely cause of Voldemort's evil is that his frontal
lobe is physically similar to that of sociopaths' brains we have studied, and
partially transfigures the "dark parts" of Voldemort's brain to light ones
- that is, he forms new, fully functioning neurons in Voldey's brain in the
right places to restore empathy, compassion for others, etc.

Further thoughts:
1. As a rationalist, Harry knows that people are not simply evil mutants, but
simply have a different perspective where their actions seem right in their own
minds. All of Voldey's patterns - the output of his utility function - are
consistent with what happens given the cocktail of certain genetic patterns
mixed with early childhood trauma - and should be physically reversible, thereby
changing the output of said function.
2. Harry knows he needs to neutralize both the Death Eaters and Voldemort;
neutralizing one or the other independently will almost certainly not suffice.
The most effective way to do both at once would be to somehow persuade
Voldemort to order the Death Eaters to stand down.
3. Harry and Hermione already proved through experimentation that they could
transfigure things that do exist, even if they do not know how to make them, in
Chapter 28.
4. Partial transfiguration is quicker than standard transfiguration via replacing
a whole, so Harry could complete this quickly - like he did with the
mountain troll.
5. This would fulfill the prophecy that Harry would "destroy" Voldemort with
a power he knew not - the destruction of course is that of Tom Riddle's
constructed persona of Voldemort and the power could be either partial
transfiguration or empathy/love.

Philosopher's Stone, and the Toms could work together from that point on
bringing about the singularity - the end of death. In this would the other
prophecy be fulfilled, the one about Harry being the end of the world and
destroying the stars - "end" in the sense of "goal" or what the universe was
ultimately heading toward in the production of humanity that could reverse the
decaying process and defeat death itself. In such a scenario, I see them
eventually destroying and reshaping stars as they saw fit in pursuit of this
goal.

transfiguration as a "power he knows not" to Voldemort while performing the
actual partial transformation, if he needs to buy himself more time. After
all, describing the mindset you have to get into in order to be able to do
partial transfiguration should help focus the mind into that state.
