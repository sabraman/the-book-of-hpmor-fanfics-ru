# Chapter 7: Confidences

<div id="seg-0265__storytext-9c93142583" class="calibre4">

As soon as Flitwick had left, Hermione grabbed Harry's hand, her face showing more excitement than Harry could remember ever seeing there before.

"Spill!" she ordered, eyes locked on his.

He glanced around, and shook his head. "Not here, Hermione. Not private enough. Nowhere near private enough. You'll understand. Um... So, I have some ideas about where we could go, but your memory is better than mine. What's the best place you can think of, where we could really talk privately?"

Hermione paused for thought, and her memory immediately answered the call. She grinned triumphantly.

"When we first arrived at Hogwarts, we came across the lake in little boats - remember, Harry?"

"Of course I remember - my memory isn't as good as yours, but it's not terrible. It never would have occurred to me, mind you, but that's beside the point."

"Well, so there's that underground harbour and then the stone steps - it comes out near the front door of the castle, though somehow I've never noticed the entrance since then. If we can get down to that cave, no one would think to look for us there. And actually, you have a phoenix - of course we can get there!"

"Brilliant! And we can hide ourselves even there - fake panels and a silencing charm. Shall we, Miss Granger?"

"Er... I do need to be released from here, Harry."

Harry grinned. "No you don't. Trust me."

Hermione nodded dubiously, and Harry sprang into action.

First he pulled the curtains around her bed again, and cast the sound-blurring charm. Then he looked carefully at his watch before grabbing Hermione's hand again. A fiery instant later, they were in the underground cavern. "We can talk here as long as we need to. After that we return to your hospital bed, pretty much the instant we left it - right now that sounds ridiculous to you, but that's one of the secrets I'll be telling you."

Hermione stared at him. "Um... OK? I trust you, Harry. Don't make me wish I hadn't."

He looped the chain of his Time-Turner around her neck as well as his, and turned the hourglass twice. She looked puzzled, but didn't argue.

Together they transfigured grains of sand into panels of fake rock, and used them to set up a small hidden space deep in the shadows partway along one edge of the cave. From inside this space Harry blended the panels together at the joins, and blended the edges into the natural rock. Hermione conjured a small green flame, and set it on a ledge to provide a dim light. Harry cast every privacy ward that he could cast - he couldn't come close to what Quirrell or Bester could do, but the little space was now protected unless an adult wizard or witch made a deliberate effort to probe that particular unremarkable section of wall.

Finally Harry sat back, satisfied. "OK, we're as safe as we can be without adult help. Which wouldn't actually make us safer unless we could completely trust the adult, come to think, so I guess we're as safe as we can be. So, what do you want to know first?"

Hermione gave him a frankly predatory look, every inch the Ravenclaw in pursuit of knowledge. "First I want to know how we'll make it back to the infirmary the moment we left. All of my books say time travel is impossible, but I don't believe that - I think at least a few students here have some way of traveling back in time, but I have no idea how."

Harry grinned, further validated in his decision to tell Hermione everything. "Yeah, they don't want a lot of people knowing. This hourglass thingie around my neck isn't really a spimster wicket like I'm supposed to say it is. Actually it's a Time Turner - each turn takes you back one hour. I took us back two hours - right now your past self is unconscious in the infirmary, and my past self is probably still arguing with Dumbledore. The reason I got this thing is to treat a sleep disorder - my days were 26 hours long, so on average I had to loop an extra two hours every day. These days I loop six hours - that's the limit, or so I'm told. I haven't tested that, because time travel frankly terrifies me. I'll explain the scientific and magical reasons for that later if you like, but it's not really the point right now. What would you like to know next?"

Hermione barely even hesitated. "Why can't I cast the Patronus, and can I see yours now?"

A moment later,

*"Expecto Patronum!"*

and Hermione was staring at a Patronus so bright that for the first few seconds it was just a dazzling blur to her. Then she saw the human shape in it, and her jaw dropped.

"That's ridiculous. Harry James Potter-Evans-Verres, do you swear to me that this really is what you say it is?"

"I swear. Brace yourself, Hermione - there are way more shocks to come. For instance, Mr Glowy Person here can destroy Dementors."

"What? No! That's impossible! Nothing can destroy... Oh, never mind. How did you know it could? Surely you didn't attack Azkaban with no plan."

"After we all practised with the Dementor that Professor Quirrell had brought here, I stayed back when everyone else went back to the castle. It was just me, Dumbledore, Quirrell and the aurors. I tried the Patronus spell again - well, my version of it this time - and it worked, and it destroyed the Dementor. Quirrell stunned the aurors and Dumbledore obliviated them, so the only people who know about it are me, Dumbledore and Quirrell. And you, now. Draco knows I can cast a Patronus, and that it's way brighter than a normal one, but that's all he knows - I made him turn his back the whole time."

Harry dispelled his Patronus, and he and Hermione sat in silence and stared blankly at the walls for a few minutes. Finally Hermione spoke.

"Can... Can I learn to do that?"

"I think so, yeah - I'll see if I can teach you."

"Thank you."

A silent minute later,

"Harry, thank you for trusting me. Thank you for telling me what you've told me already, and for promising to tell me more."

"You're welcome. Very welcome, actually - I was a bit stupid not to think of telling you earlier. I'm just so used to being alone, not having anyone I can really trust. Now that I realise I really can trust you, of course I'm going to tell you everything I can. Plus, you would not believe how good it feels to finally just be honest with someone."

Hermione smiled. "So, have you invented any other cool magic?"

"Well, you already know about Partial Transfiguration..."

Hermione nodded.

"... and you know about the potion I kinda invented for that battle, and you know about a lot of my more creative uses of existing magic."

Further nodding from Hermione, and Harry continued.

"I did invent the rocket-assisted broomstick, and yes I did nearly die the first and only time I used it, but that's part of another story. Um... I think that's about it for major magical discoveries so far - sorry, but I am still only 11."

Harry was vaguely aware that his Slytherin side was yelling at him, but he ignored it in favour of listening to Hermione.

"I know," she said. "Um... I think I know why Fawkes was on your shoulder today, but why was he on your shoulder after Bellatrix Black escaped from Azkaban?"

Harry paused.

"The Headmaster and I had a big argument about Azkaban. I said it needed to be destroyed, but he said that would harm society too much or something. You might say Fawkes took my side."

Hermione nodded slowly, and Harry stared nervously at her face. Looking up at him, she noticed sweat on his forehead and a slight flush on his face.

"Harry? What is it? There's something really big that you haven't told me yet, isn't there?"

He nodded.

"Hermione, you matter a lot to me. I think I can trust you in a way that I can't trust anyone else at all - not my parents, not Professor McGonagall, not Professor Flitwick, and definitely not Dumbledore or Quirrell. Um... Honestly, I'm terrified that you won't want to be my friend after I tell you this. What I did does look really bad - at the time I thought I was doing the right thing, but now I'm not sure. And if anyone finds out then I'm in big trouble, and not just me either. Um... I know I can't make you promise not to hate me, and I can't even make you promise not to tell anyone if you do hate what I did. But can you promise that you'll at least hear me out? Hear exactly what I did and why, and then you can judge me and do whatever you feel is right. OK?"

Hermione suddenly jumped, and bit off an exclamation. Visibly controlling herself, she nodded. "I promise I'll hear you out. I promise. But you were at Azkaban, weren't you? You broke Bellatrix Black out of Azkaban!"

Harry nodded, seeming to shrink in on himself. "Yes. I broke Bellatrix Black out of Azkaban. Well, technically I helped Professor Quirrell break Bellatrix Black out of Azkaban. And I know that doesn't sound good, but let me explain. Um...

"According to Professor Quirrell, Bellatrix Black never chose to go Dark. He says she was taken by force. Not the Imperius - I asked - but by longer-term Dark Magic for breaking and binding someone's will. Um... I kinda just trusted him on that. I know that was stupid, you don't have to tell me. He still might have been telling the truth, I don't know, but I know I was stupid to just take his word for it. Um... Though, come to think, even if he was lying, I'm still glad I took her out. Even if she's literally the most evil person alive, like Neville said, she still doesn't deserve that. No one does. Anyway, I don't know whether he was telling the truth or not. Right now a Healer somewhere on the other side of the world is trying to heal her, but she - the Healer, that is - told me not to get my hopes up. So I don't know.

"Um... And I really should tell you, I figured out afterwards that Quirrell has another motive - Bellatrix Black is the most likely person to have the lost magical knowledge of Salazar Slytherin, since Voldemort killed Slytherin's Monster and since Voldemort himself is mostly dead. So if she can be healed, Bellatrix Black is the best chance of recovering that lost knowledge."

Hermione was staring at Harry.

"And yet you have a phoenix on your shoulder," she said quietly, "so you must still be a good person."

Harry nodded. "Honestly, even if Bellatrix Black does try to do terrible things if and when she recovers, I'm probably still ahead of the game - I did destroy Azkaban, after all, or at least the bit where the prisoners were literally tortured to death. I have big plans for my life, Hermione, but even just this one thing that I did in a few minutes this morning is pretty damn good as a lifetime accomplishment. I still maybe shouldn't have broken Bellatrix out, mind you, and I definitely wouldn't have if I'd known that I was going to destroy all the Dementors later anyway, but I do still like myself."

Hermione smiled. "I still like you too, Harry. A part of me is screaming that I should be reporting you right now, but I'm not going to."

"Thank you. And thank you for still liking me."

Hermione suddenly wore a serious look. "Arguing with Dumbledore? You said your past self was probably still arguing with Dumbledore. What were you arguing about?"

"I don't think the school's safe, and I want it made safer. McGonagall and Flitwick agree with me - they were both there too - but the Headmaster just wouldn't listen. He seemed almost obsessed with me, to be honest - insisted that he couldn't possibly spare the time to update the wards until he'd dealt with the threat that he thinks I pose, and insisted that the security improvements were somehow a power grab by me. Oh, and he refuses to let McGonagall or Flitwick improve anything either. It's pretty frustrating. Although Flitwick did later tell me that I shouldn't have antagonised him - Dumbledore that is - and that I should try to negotiate more politely with him."

"So is Dumbledore a threat to you right now? I can't believe I'm saying this."

"Um... Possibly. Probably? I don't think he'd kill me outright - I'm pretty sure that'd mess with his plans - but I wouldn't put it past him to put some sort of compulsion on me. Or just lock me up, or hurt me in whatever ways for whatever reasons - I think he's terrified of me becoming a powerful Dark Lord, and he's willing to do pretty much anything to make that not happen. He tried to send me to live with my evil aunt and uncle as a child, you know, and only failed because they're not evil. Seriously, I had to pretend to him that they had abused me."

Hermione seemed rather surprised by that, but let it pass in favour of asking for details of Harry's first trip to Azkaban. He obliged at length, and she began to realise just how impressive her friend really was. Not powerful as such - she was pretty sure she had greater magical power than he did - but frighteningly good at getting things done no matter how limited his powers were. They talked about the aftermath of that adventure, and how much Harry was forced to grow up - she had noticed at the time, of course, but hadn't understood.

Eventually Hermione held up a hand, cutting Harry off in the middle of a sentence.
"Harry," she said, "what are we actually going to do? For now I think I know everything I need to about your secrets and your… well, our challenges I guess, but what are we going to do about it?"

Harry shrugged. "Honestly, I'm not sure. I feel like I'm going to have to do something drastic, but I'm worried that it might go horribly wrong in a way that I can't fix. Er, that we can't fix. Sorry, still getting used to that."

"Why don't we follow Professor Flitwick's advice, and go talk to the Headmaster?" asked Hermione. "Just you and me, I mean. Not challenging his authority or anything, but just talking and trying to compromise. I can probably stop you from challenging him. I think."

Harry sighed. "Yeah, that's probably a good idea, and thank you. I hate this. I wish I could just explain to him why I'm right and he's wrong, and that would be it. And if it turned out that he was right and I was wrong, he'd explain that to me and I'd be able to see it and we could go from there. I… I wish the world made sense, is all. But you're right - this is the world we live in, and your plan is the best we've got. Let's do that, preferably as soon as we can get you out of the infirmary."

Harry checked his watch, then started canceling charms. In a few seconds, the only sign that they'd been there was a few grains of sand on the floor. A few more seconds, and even those grains were scattered across the beach. He smiled, then took her hand so Bentham could flame them back to the infirmary. Hermione hastily slipped under the covers while Harry checked his watch.

"Perfect timing - our previous selves only left a few seconds before we got back. The barrier's still in place - I think we're fine."

Hermione nodded, and Harry canceled the sound-blurring barrier.
