# Chapter 109: The Truth, Pt 6 – Riddles Upon Riddles

“Well…” began Professor Quirrell, not taking his eyes off of the reflection, "I suppose the mirror is aptly named. Still, there is time enough for exploring what this means later. For now, reach into the mirror and grab the Stone. Remember that that stone is the key to your friend’s resurrection, and that I hold hostage the lives of hundreds of students."

Harry, still distracted by what he was seeing in the mirror did not move from under his cloak. He was very very aware that he ought to be looking for alternative courses of action. At the same time, he was also very aware that had Professor Quirrell been the one wearing the invisibility cloak, the mirror would have shown the same reflection. Maybe their goals weren’t so different after all.

The Defense Professor gestured towards the mirror with his gun. Harry reached into it and grabbed the stone. He felt an intangible jolt and the beginnings of a faint song in the back of his mind. Those feelings were all too quickly replaced by an extreme sense of doom as the stone was torn from his hand and flown into the Defense Professor’s grasp.

Professor Quirrell smiled. He then stretched out his hands invitingly and moved into a different stance, “Well boy, you saw the mirror. Vanquish me.”

Harry knew that this was his chance. It wasn’t a cold steel rod in his spine, but it was something straight and solid nonetheless. Harry grabbed it, and stabbed it into his professor. He stabbed again and again. The sense of doom was overpowering, but that only helped. Its pulsing power matched Harry’s own. It set the rhythm.

As Harry tore him apart, the Defense Professor used the stone to take various forms - first as Luna, then Draco, then Lockhart. He knew what Harry wanted because he knew what he would have wanted. The resonance in their magic also meant that even as he felt his own pleasure, he felt Harry’s as well and vice versa. The positive feedback loop was intense. He'd never known such power.

If you had been watching from outside you would have seen two twisting bodies embracing each other in front of an apparently ordinary mirror. Behind that other pane of existence, Albus Dumbledore was smiling with a strange sad look in his eyes, like someone who has taken one more step toward a foreseen destination.
