It is written,

"Now suppose I snapped my fingers, and caused all the atoms in the universe to move into positions that would be appropriate for (one probable quantum branch) of 2108. Even the atoms in your brain.

Which \*suggests\* that in a universe where timeless quantum physics is true, time travel is or at least could be simply some form of Transfiguration; and, in fact, some form of \*permanent\* Transfiguration.

If this is true, it might well explain the Time Limit. There is only a finite amount of things that can change in six hours (assuming information not travelling faster than the speed of light), and thus, correspondingly, a finite amount of material that needs to be transfigured. The time limit could simply be a measure to save power. And if time travel involves Transfiguration, that might also explain why understanding the true nature of the universe enables \*partial\* Transfiguration, a fact which otherwise, given that magic is usually quite content to use the Potioneer's Principle of Conservation and Aristotelian-physics broomsticks, seems quite bizarre.

And here there is a Philosopher's Stone, capable of Permanent Transfiguration on its very own. Perhaps it could be used as an outside power source for the time travel, and thus remove or at least \*extend\* the six-hour time limit. Less than a year is still not all that much space, in terms of light-distance, in terms of the universe as a whole...

Voldemort certainly seems to have a strong term in his utility function for Harry having a Hermione whose cautions he'll listen to! And who else?
