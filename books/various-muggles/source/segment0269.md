# Chapter 11: Reacquaintance

<div id="seg-0269__storytext-9c93142583" class="calibre4">

The moment Harry and Hermione arrived in the Great Hall with Bentham, they were met by Professor Quirrell. Inclining his head slightly, he addressed Hermione.

"Miss Granger, might I have a moment of your boyfriend's time?"

This left Hermione flustered for a number of reasons, not all of them anticipated by Quirrell, but Harry intervened before she could gather her wits enough to say anything at all.

"I'm afraid that's not going to happen, Professor Quirrell. Put simply, right now I'm not letting Hermione out of my sight for any reason. I very nearly lost her today, and I'm finally taking security seriously."

Quirrell raised an eyebrow. "Really, Mr Potter? You believe yourself so much more capable than Miss Granger, that your mere presence within sight of her is both necessary and sufficient for her safety? I fear you are forgetting your lessons."

Harry flinched, he couldn't help that, but he had expected this. He paused for a moment, face carefully neutral as he considered his response.

"I do have weaknesses, Professor - everyone has weaknesses, I haven't forgotten that. I do think Hermione's a lot safer with me than on her own, though, partly because I lack some of her weaknesses and partly because most dangers are a lot less severe for two people working together than for one person working alone. It goes the other way too, don't forget - I know that I've just painted a big target on myself by what I did today, and I think I'm much safer with Hermione than on my own."

"And in that, Mr Potter, you most definitely have not learned the larger lesson. While I intend no disrespect to Miss Granger's school performance, nor even to her performance as a general, she is now unquestionably a liability to you. Your enemies can now hurt you by hurting her."

Harry shoved his temper back down, still keeping his face carefully neutral. This was a crucial contest, and he couldn't afford to lose.

"With respect, sir, that was already the case. I am not now choosing for Hermione's well-being to affect my own - in reality, I'm just finally taking appropriate steps in response to that admitted vulnerability. Honestly sir, after today's Wizengamot session, isn't it painfully obvious to everyone how much Hermione matters to me?"

Ice-blue eyes met furious green.

"That, Mr Potter, is precisely my point. You have acknowledged a weakness, and yet your only response is to raise the stakes without adding more than token safeguards. You are not thinking clearly, Mr Potter, and your sentimental foolishness will inevitably cost you a great deal. When it does, do not say that I did not warn you."

Harry shrugged. "Your argument is not new to me, Professor. I disagree, as you are well aware. Now, what did you want to talk about? Hermione already knows what happened today, so there shouldn't be anything that she shouldn't hear."

Harry noticed a brief flicker in Quirrell's mask in response to that, a fleeting glance towards Hermione. Oops. A moment later, Quirrell turned to address Hermione. His eyes met hers earnestly, but then he flinched involuntarily before he could even start to speak. An awkward silence fell, as all three of them rapidly considered their options. Harry spoke first, outrage perfectly clear in the tone and volume of his voice. This needed to be overheard.

"What the hell do you think you're doing, Professor Quirrell? You just tried to rape my friend's mind! Did you really think you could get away with that? That we wouldn't notice, or that we wouldn't do anything about it?"

Quirrell replied more quietly, though still loudly enough for a few nearby people to hear. "This is not a discussion for the Great Hall, Mr Potter. Shall we move it to my office? I can have food sent."

Harry shook his head furiously. Knowing that almost the entire Hall would be staring at him by now, he quickly summoned Dumbledore and McGonagall with a glance and a gesture. As he turned back to Quirrell, he noted fleetingly (and with considerable relief) that Professor Flitwick was already striding towards him. Taking a deep breath, Harry once more addressed Quirrell and the Hall at large.

"No, Professor Quirrell. This is not a discussion for closed doors and political bargaining. It has to be public."

At this point Professor Flitwick arrived, wand drawn but not aimed. His voice rang out high and clear, with no trace of its usual self-deprecating jollity.

"What's the problem, Mr Potter?"

Harry, wand likewise in hand and voice practically a snarl, answered Flitwick without taking his eyes off Quirrell.

"*Professor* Quirrell here just attempted Legilimency on Hermione."

The tiny Charms professor showed the sheer shock of it, but even as he flinched he was suddenly pointing his wand at Quirrell. Dumbledore and McGonagall, arriving at this point, followed his example. They both kept their eyes and wands on Quirrell as Dumbledore addressed Harry.

"Harry?"

Harry carefully softened his voice. "He tried to read Hermione's mind, Headmaster."

The Headmaster wasn't particularly shocked by this, of course, knowing that Severus Snape had been doing the same thing to many students for years. On the other hand, he knew how Harry felt about the practice, and he also knew the law. It was a bit early in the year to be losing the Defence Professor already, especially such an extraordinarily competent one, but Harry's obvious fury made the choice a simple one.

"Would you like me to call the Aurors back, Harry?"

Regret was clear on Harry's face, but he nodded without hesitation.

"Yes please, Headmaster."

Quirrell twirled his wand nonchalantly, a mocking smile on his face. "Am I to be arrested again so soon, then, and on the word of a child?"

Dumbledore was busy sending his Patronus to fetch the Aurors, so it was McGonagall who answered.

"Yes, Quirinus. I'm afraid you are. While we cannot prove your involvement in the incident involving Miss Granger and Mr Malfoy, we certainly can do something about your attempt to Legilimise Miss Granger."

He spread his hands. "What evidence have you seen, other than Mr Potter's bare accusation? Is it not more plausible that Mr Potter is merely looking for a villain to explain away his girlfriend's misdeeds, and has settled on me as an easy target? The accusation itself is hardly plausible, in any case - even if I had attempted to access Miss Granger's mind, how exactly could these children have detected and correctly identified the attempt?"

Flitwick stepped forward half a pace, wand still steadily aimed at the centre of Quirrell's chest. "The details can wait until the Aurors arrive, but I vouch for their abilities in this regard."

Quirrell's face became a blank mask of concentration, his body a coiled spring. His wand was now firmly in hand. Flitwick began to drift around to Quirrell's right, and McGonagall to his left. Both looked tense and more than a little nervous, but their wands remained aimed and ready as they moved. Harry quietly slipped behind Dumbledore and backed away a half-dozen paces, pulling Hermione with him. If this became a fight, it would be far above their level - they would be liabilities, plain and simple. He exchanged a glance and a nod with Bentham on his shoulder, but raised his strongest shield regardless - it might buy them a vital split-second, if the situation went badly. Hermione held his hand tightly.

Elsewhere in the Hall, the more intelligent prefects were quietly herding students away from the developing confrontation.

The silence held.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

It was to this silence that the Auror trio returned, slipping quietly into the Hall. They took in the situation at a glance; Komodo made a quick gesture as he entered, and Butnaru and Goryanof drifted away either side of him as the three rapidly closed the distance to the looming conflict. Only when they were in position around Quirrell, forming a second ring in the gaps left by the professors, did Komodo finally speak.

"What's the allegation, and what's the evidence?"

The professors glanced at each other, wondering who should speak, but Harry spoke up first.

"Attempted Legilimency on Hermione Granger, right here in the Great Hall. The immediate evidence is my word and Hermione's, but we'll have plenty more for when it goes to trial."

Auror Komodo glanced at Albus Dumbledore. "Do you vouch for this accusation, Chief Warlock?"

Dumbledore nodded, never taking his eyes off Quirrell. "I do, Auror Komodo. I have never yet known Harry Potter to tell a lie, and the mere idea of Hermione Granger doing so is absurd. If they both say that Quirinus Quirrell attempted Legilimency upon Miss Granger, then Quirinus Quirrell attempted Legilimency upon Miss Granger."

Komodo nodded grimly. "Thank you, sir. Quirinus Quirrell, you are under arrest for attempted Legilimency against Hermione Granger. Drop your wand and place your hands on your head."

Quirrell sank smoothly into a crouch, all cold precision and tight-leashed power. Behind him Professor Flitwick exchanged glances with Aurors Butnaru and and Goryanoff, and Butnaru began to count down silently on his fingers.

Suddenly Quirrell exploded into action, a highly choreographed dance performed at lightning speed. He turned fractionally, silently throwing out a rapidly-expanding bludgeoning shield in all directions. In the split-second that this bought him, he leapt towards Minerva McGonagall. An unidentified spell fired behind him sent him flying over her head, and a rapid-fire stream of simple bludgeoners sent her to her knees as he flew overhead. He landed and rolled, flying out of the roll even faster than he had entered it. His wand was now flickering in his hand, scattering a hail of curses around the Hall as he dodged or deflected the few spells that his pursuers managed to fire, and he was running far faster than any human body could manage without magical help. The ancient double doors were shut and locked - Komodo was no fool - but with a twitch of Quirrell's wand they blew away in a cloud of splinters.

The massive front doors, closed but not locked, fared no better a few seconds later. As those once-mighty slabs left the castle as a shock-wave of debris, Quirrell scattered curses into the stonework above the gaping hole they left behind. He crossed the threshold horizontal and still accelerating, clearing it less than a second before the first massive stone smashed down onto the flagstones. Beyond that he was simply gone, obscured and protected from his pursuers by a cascade of collapsing masonry and a choking cloud of dust.
