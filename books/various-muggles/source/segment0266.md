# Chapter 8: Questions

<div id="seg-0266__storytext-9c93142583" class="calibre4">

Only a few minutes after Harry and Hermione had returned to the infirmary, the curtain was pulled back and Dumbledore stepped in. The Headmaster smiled genially at Hermione.

"How are you feeling, Miss Granger?"

"Much better," she said. "Can I leave the infirmary now?"

Dumbledore looked thoughtful for a moment, then nodded.

"Very well, unless Madam Pomfrey has a specific reason to keep you here. However, you must return here immediately if you feel faint or ill."

Hermione readily agreed to that, of course.

"Headmaster," Harry said after a moment, "I think I made a mistake earlier today, in taking such a confrontational approach to you. I apologise for that – if you can spare the time, would you be willing to meet with Hermione and me some time today for a much friendlier chat?"

Dumbledore gave him a searching look, but then nodded.

"Certainly, Harry, certainly. If you would both like to follow me, we can go to my office right now."

Harry nodded. Fawkes flickered across to Dumbledore's shoulder, and the old man positively beamed. A moment later he and Fawkes vanished. Hermione stood up and reached out to take Harry's hand, but stopped abruptly and strode instead to Madam Pomfrey's office.

"Madam Pomfrey, may I go now? I'm perfectly fine, and Professor Dumbledore wants to see me in his office now."

Madam Pomfrey glared at the girl before her, but could see nothing obviously wrong with her.

"Very well, but you're to come straight back here if you feel faint."

"Yes, Madam Pomfrey."

Harry took her hand, and Bentham flamed the two of them directly into the Headmaster's office.

"Headmaster, you say that you aren't willing to upgrade the wards until you're satisfied that I'm not a threat to the Wizarding world. What can I do to reassure you on that point?"

Dumbledore looked up at the boy and sighed.

"I'm afraid it's not so simple as that, Harry. I doubt neither your intentions nor your sincerity in this moment, so no mere words from you can reassure me. You are dangerous, young man, and all the more so as you cannot see it."

"Wait," said Hermione. "Doesn't that mean that Harry could reassure you by acknowledging how dangerous he is?"

Dumbledore stared at her for a moment, then inclined his head to her.

"That would certainly be a start."

Harry stepped forward.

"OK, Professor. I want to change the world, and I think I can. That has to mean I also have the power to break it. So, we agree on that. I think the difference is that I'm sure I wouldn't do that, but you think I might go Dark. Or maybe that I already am Dark, I'm not sure. I definitely think quite differently to you, so I can see where you're coming from."

Dumbledore nodded, and Harry went on.

"I can't really explain how my worldview stops me from going Dark, because you can't understand it well enough for that. Um... I probably should have planned this a bit better. Give me a moment..."

Long seconds later, Harry suddenly jumped.

"Of course! Headmaster, I have the perfect assurance for you: Hermione! I can promise you right now that I won't ever do anything that she tells me not to do. And... Um, this bit itself is a pretty big secret..."

"I will keep your confidences, Mr Potter. Carry on."

"Well, Hermione knows my secrets now. She's the only person who does, and that's how it's going to stay. But if you trust her to be good, then you can trust me to be good. Or at least not to do anything seriously bad."

Dumbledore looked genuinely shocked.

"Harry, I know you to possess secrets which could do a great deal of damage if they were to be exposed. While I would not question Miss Granger's integrity, have you not considered the possibility that someone might violate her mental privacy? You may have learned Occlumency, but to my knowledge she has not."

Harry closed his eyes for a few moments as he consciously calmed himself down - it was a reasonable point, the headmaster couldn't reasonably have anticipated Flitwick's solution, and getting angry would be neither reasonable nor helpful.

"Hermione's mind is protected by different defences, Headmaster. You're right - I didn't dare tell her my secrets until I was sure she couldn't simply have them plucked out of her head."

Dumbledore frankly stared at him. "You have proved me wrong before, young man, but once more I find myself caught in sheer disbelief. Can you tell me the nature of this protection?"

Hermione cut in. "It's a charm that Professor Flitwick cast - I think he might have invented it on the spot, or at least modified an existing spell when I asked him. It's probably simpler to demonstrate than to explain - would you like to try to read my mind, Headmaster, in order to see for yourself what happens? He did describe the experience as 'a sharp slap', though."

Dumbledore looked thoughtfully at Hermione, then nodded slightly. A moment later, he flinched. His jaw dropped, and for long seconds he frankly stared. Finally he nodded his head slowly. "Very well - your mind certainly is well-protected, Miss Granger. And Harry, I take your point - whilever you are bound by Miss Granger's morality, you are indeed barred from that seductive path into the darkness."

"Thank you," said Harry fervently.

"As to the wards," said Dumbledore, "I shall undertake a thorough review as soon as I am able. I shall bear your suggestions in mind, and implement as many as are reasonable."

Harry stared at him.

"Roughly what sort of timescale are we talking about here, Headmaster?"

"Perhaps a few months for the initial assessment, a few months to plan the upgrades, and a week or two to actually carry out the changes."

Harry took a half-step forward.

"Headmaster, people will die! Remember, Draco very nearly did die - that's why we're here. I don't want any of my classmates to die."

"You exaggerate, Mr Potter. I shall excuse it as a failing of youth, but you really ought to restrain yourself."

Hermione laid a hand on Harry's shoulder, and he subsided.

"Professor Dumbledore," said Hermione, "could I be involved in that? I don't suppose I'll be able to add much as a first-year, but this is a priceless opportunity for me to see exactly how serious wards are set up. Er, and from the look he's giving me I think Harry wants the same."

The Headmaster paused, and for several seconds he even closed his eyes. When his eyes opened, his face showed the full strain of his responsibilities.

"Miss Granger, above all else I must protect this world. You wield power already, and in time I suspect you will wield a great deal more. Will you first make an Unbreakable Vow to never abandon the Light?"

"Of course, Professor."

Dumbledore's wand was suddenly in his hand, without Harry or Hermione having seen him move to draw it. He turned and strode to the fireplace, igniting it with a twitch of his wand and tossing in a pinch of green powder from a bowl on the mantel. "Head of Ravenclaw office," he said rapidly but precisely, and a moment later his head had disappeared into the flames. Harry, meanwhile, was suddenly reminded of just how powerful and competent the Headmaster really was with magic. He repeated to himself his initial assessment, from the Welcoming Feast a lifetime ago: "Don't mess with Dumbledore."

A moment later Dumbledore withdrew his head from the fire, returning to his desk as if nothing had happened. Another moment, and Filius Flitwick emerged from the fire with the same graceful twirl that Harry remembered from McGonagall's office. Hermione, who was seeing it for the first time, looked at her Head of House with increased respect (which was saying something, this being Hermione).

"Filius," said Dumbledore, "I have agreed to upgrade our wards. Miss Granger wishes to be involved in the process, and I wish from her an Unbreakable Vow for security. Will you act as Bonder?"

Filius glanced at Hermione for confirmation, and then nodded. Dumbledore knelt, and motioned Hermione to do likewise. He took her hand in his, and then Flitwick's wand rested on their joined hands as Dumbledore began to speak very carefully.

"Will you, Hermione, remain always on the side of Light?"

"I will."

A thin tongue of brilliant flame sprang from Flitwick's wand and wound its way around their hands like a red-hot wire.

"Will you always guard any knowledge you gain about the wards of Hogwarts, and never divulge it to others or use it against Hogwarts?"

"I will."

A somewhat thicker tongue of flame leapt from Flitwick's wand, interweaving itself with the first.

"So be it."

The flames tightened around their joined hands, leaving thin red welts behind as they sank into the skin and vanished.

Hermione stood up, but Dumbledore remained kneeling.

"Harry, are you willing to make a Vow also? I will ask only that you not act against Hermione's wishes in significant matters of conscience."

Harry caught himself flinching away from making the commitment, and brutally overrode that flinch - all he was doing here was formalising the promise he had already made to Hermione, and even a brief hesitation at this point could carry significant costs. He knelt down and took Dumbledore's hand.

"Will you, Harry, always be bound by Hermione's morality in major matters of conscience?"

"I will."

As before, a thin tongue of flame from Flitwick's wand.

"So be it," said Dumbledore, a look of profound relief on his ancient and careworn face.

Harry winced slightly as the flames cut into his hand, but smiled beatifically nonetheless.
