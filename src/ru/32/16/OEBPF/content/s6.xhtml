Chapter 6: Rethink

As the other students filed quietly out of the classroom, mostly seeming to be lost in thought, Harry and the phoenixes stayed behind with Professor McGonagall. She gave him a questioning look, but he flicked his eyes briefly to the departing students and said nothing. She nodded faintly and waited.

When the last of the other students had left the room and gone out of sight down the corridor, Harry walked over and closed the door. As he walked back toward Minerva, she cast a few privacy charms - she did so almost absently, Harry noted.

"Thanks for letting me speak to the class - I think that few minutes might really make a difference. Sorry if it ends up getting you into trouble with the Headmaster."

Minerva shrugged slightly. "I've made my choice, Mr Potter. All I ask is that you don't make me regret it."

Harry nodded seriously. "Of course. Cutting class to go see Hermione now wouldn't count as that, would it?"

Minerva shook her head. "Not at all, young man, not at all. By all means go and see your friend - I rather suspect she'll need you."

Harry turned and almost ran towards the door, as Minerva hastily canceled her wards to allow him out. She watched him go, a faint smile upon her ancient face.

Once outside the door, Harry paused for a moment before flickering and disappearing. A moment later he stood at the doors of the infirmary, feeling faintly stupid for not having planned to use phoenix travel in the first place.

Madam Pomfrey strode over as Harry entered.

"Shouldn't you be in class, young man?"

Harry's Gryffindor side favoured direct honesty, confident that Madam Pomfrey would be sympathetic. Slytherin outright laughed at this, and it was left to Ravenclaw to point out to Gryffindor that Madam Pomfrey was perhaps not quite so sympathetic and adaptable as all that. After a moment's reflection, even Gryffindor had to concede the point.

"I just wanted to see my best friend, Madam Pomfrey. She's been hurt very badly in the last day or two, and I really want to be here for her when she wakes up. Professor McGonagall said it was OK."

Madam Pomfrey subsided slightly. "Very well. Just don't let her get excited - she needs her rest."

"I'll do what I can."

He walked quickly to the bed where Hermione lay, seeming to relax slightly as he drew near to her. He took a seat next to her bed, and gazed absently at her face.

He suddenly understood Lucius Malfoy's promise about Draco's wellbeing - Harry himself now knew that he felt much the same way about Hermione. However he might justify it in utilitarian terms, that was nothing more than post-hoc rationalisation - the truth was that he would protect her with his life because in his world her loss was unthinkable. He had nearly lost her, had been incredibly lucky not to lose her - he had risked his life in Azkaban and achieved an insanely improbable victory in order to save her. And the thing about improbable victories was that you couldn't make a habit of them, because in the real world you'd usually just lose. He needed to shift the odds a long way in his and her favour, and other considerations suddenly seemed a lot less important.

As he emerged from this contemplation, he became aware of Professor Flitwick standing quietly on the other side of the bed. The tiny man inclined his head to Harry.

"I came straight here from Albus' office, and have been with her ever since. She hasn't stirred."

"Thank you, Professor. I'd have been here myself, but I needed to be in Transfiguration."

Flitwick paused at that, and looked thoughtfully at Harry.

"I hadn't wondered at your absence until now, but I see that there's more to this. Why exactly did you need to be in Transfiguration?"

"Well, for a start I didn't want to let Professor McGonagall down. But you're right, there is more - mostly that I really needed the chance to talk to my fellow students about what just happened, and the sooner the better. Pre-empting rumours is helpful, of course, but the main thing is that I'm possibly going to be moving against the Headmaster quite soon - I need support for that."

Filius looked sharply at Harry. "What exactly do you mean by moving against the Headmaster, may I ask? I never agreed to support a revolution."

"Oh no, nothing like that! Um... Look, I'll try to be precise here. I don't intend to displace Albus Dumbledore from his current position as Headmaster. I just want to apply enough pressure to make him actually listen to our concerns about security in Hogwarts."

Filius sighed. "I have known Albus Dumbledore for a very long time, Harry. I now believe that it was a mistake for us to meet him head-on in his office earlier today, and I assure you that your current plans will merely confirm him in his current position. You can try to reassure him that you yourself are not about to destroy the world - that might help. You can try to remove him as Headmaster - that would work if you managed it, though there would be consequences for such an action. What you cannot do, I'm afraid, is to openly force the greatest wizard of our time to follow a course of action which he has decided is unwise. Put simply, Mr Potter, you cannot win a dominance contest against Albus Dumbledore. I advise you not to try, and I warn you that I will not support you in any such challenge."

Harry frowned. "I see your point - I'll have to think about that. Would you recommend any particular course of action, Professor?"

Filius shrugged. "Go to see him, alone, and negotiate. Be ready to make concessions. I will help you with anything to make the castle safer, but I'm afraid I can't change the Headmaster's mind."

"Thank you. I'll do that, and I'll try very very hard not to get into a dominance contest."

Filius nodded. "Yes, do that. I wish you luck. And now, I believe that Miss Granger would prefer to see you rather than me when she wakes - if Madam Pomfrey asks, you have my permission to be here. If you need me, touch your wand to this and I'll return immediately."

He tossed Harry a Sickle, then left quietly.

Some time later, Hermione finally stirred. Her eyes fell on Harry, and she smiled tentatively and held out her hand. He took it and returned her smile. They sat in silence for a time, before Harry finally broke the silence.

"So how do you feel?"

"Surprisingly good, actually. I'm completely exhausted, of course, but nothing actually hurts."

"That's good."

"I probably should still feel guilty about Draco, even if you're right about what actually happened, but somehow I just can't make myself care."

"Well that's good, because you shouldn't be feeling bad about that anyway. I mean honestly, you'd be demanding way too much of yourself if you really felt bad just because you didn't immediately face the music when you believed that you'd done something really big and scary. You're a human being, Hermione, and probably the most moral person I've ever met - you feel OK because no one's seriously hurt and you didn't actually do anything very terrible. You failed in one small and totally understandable way, and you can learn from that, but in the end everyone's OK. Seem fair?"

She nodded. "OK, fair enough. So what now? Where do we go from here?"

"Well, the other students are probably just going to forget about this - to most of them you're probably still guilty, but what I did is so big and strange that it'll bury that. Beyond that, I really want you protected from Legilimency so that I can tell you my secrets. I'm finished with keeping things from you, at least where I have a choice."

Her eyebrows shot up. "Um... Wow. Thanks? I think? Um... Well, I've read about Occlumency and I don't think that I could do it - I'm way too young, and I really don't think my mind is… is twisty enough to do that kind of pretending. I don't know if there's another way. I'll research that, if you like."

He nodded, and she went on. "But Harry, why do you suddenly want to tell me your secrets? And what secrets have you been keeping from me, anyway?"

Harry stood up and pulled the privacy curtain around Hermione's bed, casting the sound-blurring charm as he did so. He sat now on Hermione's bed rather than on the chair, leaning in close and speaking softly.

"Hermione, I know a lot of secrets. Some of the things I know could even get people killed - believe me, I have good reason to guard my secrets. That's why I did learn Occlumency - there are people in this school who read students' minds, and these are not people that I trust with all my secrets."

Harry paused for a moment, and looked directly into Hermione's eyes. "As for why I suddenly want to tell you... Hermione, I nearly lost you today, and it scared me. Terrified me. It made me think about the gap you'd leave in my life, and what you mean to me. And then I realised that, if I value you this much, I really should tell you a whole lot more and work with you much more as an equal. And no, I am not planning to make Draco a similar offer. I'll admit that I like him and I do genuinely want to be friends with him as well as redeeming him, but there's no way I could trust him the way I trust you."

Hermione nodded slowly. "So you're promising to tell me all of your secrets, or at least most of them, as soon as you're sure no one can steal them out of my head?"

"Pretty much, yeah. You'll need to trust me, though - there are things you won't like, things that will make you think differently about me. Things that would make you sure that I actually had been sucked into the darkness like you said I was, except that I've got two phoenixes on my shoulders right now and you have some idea what that means. Um… So, are you willing to deal with that?"

She nodded. "Yes, of course, though I might be angry at first. But really, you're asking Ravenclaw me whether or not I want knowledge. Think about that."

"True - that was a bit silly of me. Hmm… Professor Flitwick was here before you woke up, and he left me a coin to call him with if I needed him. Do you mind if I call him back and ask him about ways to protect you from Legilimency?"

Hermione waved a hand impatiently. "Yes, of course - good idea."

Harry tapped the Sickle with his wand, and then canceled the sound-blurring charm and swept away the curtain around Hermione's bed. A few moments later, a slightly out-of-breath Filius Flitwick entered the infirmary and walked briskly over to Harry and Hermione.

"How are you feeling now, Miss Granger?"

"Fine, thank you Professor, and I apologise for making you rush back here."

He waved a hand dismissively, and Hermione continued.

"Professor, other than Occlumency, do you know of any effective defence against Legilimency?"

Flitwick looked surprised for a moment, but then realised. "Oh of course, that would have prevented this whole mess in the first place. Hmm…"

He sat down absently, face intent, and sat silently for several minutes (Harry noted approvingly that he spent actual whole minutes, by the clock, thinking about the problem). Eventually Flitwick looked up, face serious.

"I have a solution, Miss Granger, but there is a drawback. The best plan I can think of at present will simply block your sight any time you are subject to Legilimency. This will leave you somewhat helpless against other attacks, although the darkness will lift as soon as you turn away. Either way, it should at the very least ensure that your secrets cannot be stolen without your noticing. We can take other precautions to guard your general safety, of course."

Hermione and Harry both looked extremely curious at that, but Hermione was fractionally quicker to speak. "How does it work?"

"In short, Legilimency requires eye contact. I will cast a ward on you, anchored in your eyeballs, which will temporarily conjure an opaque barrier over each of your pupils if Legilimency is detected. This is new and untried, but I expect that from a Legilimens' point of view it will look and feel like a new type of Occlumency barrier. Do you wish to try it, Miss Granger?"

She nodded nervously. He aimed his wand at each of her eyes in turn, and muttered a lengthy incantation which neither Hermione nor Harry could even begin to parse. Hermione's pupils glowed white for a moment, and she flinched but said nothing.

Flitwick lowered his wand. "I'm not a particularly good Legilimens myself, never having practiced the art, but my skills should suffice for testing. May I, Miss Granger?"

She nodded, and stared bravely into his eyes. A moment later, both Hermione and Professor Flitwick recoiled.

"My word!" said Professor Flitwick. "I'm not quite sure why, but that actually hurt! I'd call that a rousing success, at least from my perspective - anyone who tries to invade your mind in future will be thrown out with a sharp slap. Did that test hurt you at all, Hermione?"

Hermione shook her head. "I was just surprised, that's all. Everything went black, very suddenly. It only lasted a split-second, though - it's just a shock when it happens."

"That's even better," Harry said. "You don't even need to turn away to clear your eyes, so you'll be much safer than we thought. Professor Flitwick, thank you very much."

Hermione hastened to add her own thanks, profoundly grateful to be safe inside her own head.

The tiny man bowed gracefully, a smile visible in his puffy white beard, before taking his leave.
