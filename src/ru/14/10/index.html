Chapter 10a

 

... I do know this: If you go to Ravenclaw or Slytherin, it will strengthen your coldness. If you go to Hufflepuff or Gryffindor, it will strengthen your warmth. THAT is something I care about a great deal, and it was what I wanted to talk to you about this whole time!"

The words dropped into Harry's thought processes with a shock that stopped him in his tracks. That made it sound like the obvious response was that he shouldn't go to Ravenclaw. But he belonged in Ravenclaw! Anyone could see that! He had to go to Ravenclaw!

"No, you don't," the Hat said patiently, as if it could remember a statistical summary of this part of the conversation having happened a great many previous times.

Hermione's in Ravenclaw!

Again the sense of patience. "You can meet her after lessons and work with her then."

But my plans -

"So replan! Don't let your life be steered by your reluctance to do a little extra thinking. You know that."

Where would I go, if not Ravenclaw?

"Ahem. 'Clever kids in Ravenclaw, evil kids in Slytherin, wannabe heroes in Gryffindor, and everyone who does the actual work in Hufflepuff.' This indicates a certain amount of respect. You are well aware that Conscientiousness is just about as important as raw intelligence in determining life outcomes, you think you will be extremely loyal to your friends if you ever have some, you are not frightened by the expectation that your chosen scientific problems may take decades to solve -"

I'm lazy! I hate work! Hate hard work in all its forms! Clever shortcuts, that's all I'm about!

"And you would find loyalty and friendship in Hufflepuff, a camaraderie that you have never had before. You would find that you could rely on others, and that would heal something inside you that is broken."

Again it was a shock. But what would the Hufflepuffs find in me, who never belonged in their House? Acid words, cutting wit, disdain for their inability to keep up with me?

Now it was the Hat's thoughts that were slow, hesitant. "I must Sort for the good of all the students in all the Houses... but I think you could learn to be a good Hufflepuff, and not too out of place there. You will be happier in Hufflepuff than in any other house; that is the truth."

Happiness is not the most important thing in the world to me. I would not become all that I could be, in Hufflepuff. I would sacrifice my potential.

The Hat flinched; it was long moments before it responded.

You have already achieved your greatest potential, you are the Boy-Who-Lived, you brought down the Dark Lord Voldemort. What possible acts would be even more great, more fulfilling? Your place in history is already secure, no matter what you might do. This doesn’t have to be the beginning of your journey, it is already the end. If you go to Hufflepuff, you can rebuild your family, gain friends and heal the part of you that was broken.

 

That stopped Harry. But not for long. My life is not a story, I don’t want my life defined by events I can’t even remember, the actions of a child. Whatever happened was luck, a fluke, and I want my own life, not a story chosen without my say.

 

The Hat’s response was almost a whisper. “Do you think that you are the first potential Dark Lord to pass under my brim? I cannot speak of the others to you, but I can know this: Of those who did not intend evil from the very beginning, some of them listened to my warnings, and went to Houses where they would find happiness. And some of them... some of them did not.”

 

This reply did not satisfy Harry. And of those who did not heed the warning - did they all become Dark? Or did some of them achieve greatness for good, as well? Just what are the exact percentages here, what priors do you know?

 

“I can not give you exact statistics. I cannot know them so I cannot count them. I only know a vague summary of my past experiences, and I know from this, your chances don’t feel good. They feel very not-good.”

 

But I just wouldn’t become Dark! Ever!

 

“I know I have heard that claim before. And I can tell when you are lying to yourself.”

 

But I am not Dark...

 

The Hat interrupted Harry’s rebuttal, “I can read your thoughts as they form, and can see the shape of them. I know the same information as you. And I have a summary of past experiences from others who have gone under my brim. In short, I have more information than you. As you might phrase it, I have better priors. You know this too! You know I can not sort you falsely. You should trust my judgement.”

 

Harry’s face twisted in bitterness. Or it tried to, he felt the sensation, but his facial expression remain fixed on the outside. The sensation and input from his face clashed, pausing his thoughts, until he could regroup. Look, I believe you mean well, but I am not interested in house of hard work and friendship.

 

“Yes, I do mean well. I was made by the founders of Hogwarts after all. But you don’t mean well. I know your thoughts and from what I have already seen so far, you already far down the path of a Dark Lord. Too far, maybe. You ask for priors, so look at the evidence! You bullied Neville Longbottom this morning. You knew you had a good excuse, and you thought the Boy-Who-Lived could get away with it.

 

That’s unfair! I worried that might be the case, but in the end I decided the experience would probably work to help Neville!

 

“No. That was a rationalisation. I can tell. Even you can tell, even if you want to deny the truth of it. What about your plan with the gold and silver at Gringotts?”

 

There is absolutely nothing wrong with improving the economic situation of the wizarding world! And who better than me?

 

“Yet have you considered the consequences your actions would have to wizardkind? You would destroy the savings of many innocents, disrupt the careful political world that you know nothing about. Does this seem like the kind of act a good person would do?”

 

Not every change is an improvement; but every improvement is a change, Harry answered.

 

“Cached wisdom”, the Hat retorted.

 

Frustration was building up inside Harry. He wasn't used to being outgunned in arguments, at all, ever, let alone by a Hat that could borrow all of his own knowledge and intelligence to argue with him and could watch his thoughts as they formed.  It doesn’t matter if the thought was cached, if it applies to the situation and is true!

 

“Is it? What about your thought to line up all the blood purists and guillotine them? Is that a thought that good would produce?”

 

It was a like a hard punch to Harry’s entire self. He fell back, rallied:

 

Then I won’t do that! I will be extra careful not to turn evil!

 

“Heard it. How, exactly will you accomplish that? Doubling down on good is not the same as being not-evil. And being not-evil is still not even accomplishing good.”

(Meanwhile, in the Hall beyond, a silence that had grown shallower at first as the background whispers increased, and then deepened as the whispers gave up and died away, falling finally into an utter silence that no one dared disturb with a single word, as Harry stayed under the Hat for long, long minutes, longer than all the previous first-years put together, longer than anyone in living memory. At the Head Table, Dumbledore went on smiling benignly; small metallic sounds occasionally came from Snape's direction as he idly compacted the twisted remains of what had once been a heavy silver wine goblet; and Minerva McGonagall clenched the podium in a white-knuckled grip, knowing that Harry Potter's contagious chaos had somehow infected the Sorting Hat itself and the Hat was about to, to demand that a whole new House of Doom be created just to accommodate Harry Potter or something, and Dumbledore would make her do it...)

 Just what kind of statistical summary do your 'feelings' come from, anyway? Do they take into account that I come from an Enlightenment culture, or were these other potential Dark Lords the children of spoiled Dark Age nobility, who didn't know squat about the historical lessons of how Lenin and Hitler actually turned out, or about the evolutionary psychology of self-delusion, or the value of self-awareness and rationality, or -

"No, of course they were not in this new reference class which you have just now constructed in such a way as to contain only yourself. And of course others have pleaded their own exceptionalism, just as you are doing now. But why is it necessary? Do you think that you are the last potential wizard of Light in the world? Why must you be the one to try for greatness, when I have advised you that you are riskier than average? Let some other, safer candidate try!"

But the prophecy...

“It was originally a wild guess on your part, or to be more precise, a wild joke, and McGonagall could have been reacting only to the part about the Dark Lord still being alive. You have essentially no idea of what the prophecy says or even if there is one. You're just speculating, or to put it more exactly, wishing that you have some ready-made heroic role that is your personal property."

But I'm the one who defeated him last time!

"You already said it yourself; that was a wild fluke. You don’t seriously believe that a one-year-old child had an inherent propensity to defeat Dark Lords which has been maintained ten years later. None of this is your real reason and you know it!"

The answer to this was something that Harry would not regularly have said out loud, in conversation he would have danced around it and found some more socially palatable arguments to the same conclusion -

"You think that you are potentially the greatest who has yet lived, the strongest servant of the Light, that no other is likely to take up your wand if you lay it down."

Well... yeah, frankly. I don't usually come out and say it like that, but yeah. No point in softening it, you can read my mind anyway.

"To the extent you really believe that... you must equally believe that you could be the most terrible Dark Lord the world has ever known."

Destruction is always easier than creation. Easier to tear things apart, to disrupt, than to put them back together again. If I have the potential to accomplish good on a massive scale, I must also have the potential to accomplish still greater evil... But I won't do that.

"Already you insist on risking it! Why are you so driven? What is the real reason you must not go to Hufflepuff and be happier there? What is your true fear?"

I must achieve my full potential. If I don't I... fail...

 

“And I say again, what potential could you achieve that is greater than defeating Lord Voldemort? What might be greater than the Boy-Who-Lived? Even if you defeat another Dark Lord, the accomplishment will be diminished by being second. Any greatness you achieve will be secondary, always lesser. But if you fail, if you become a Dark Lord, then whatever evil deeds you do, they will be heightened by the fact that you could have been good. Why risk this now? You needn’t fear achieving your full potential. Let go of your fears and just live the rest of your life out!”

 

It was terribly unfair, Harry decided, to have your greatest acts occur before you could remember them. Decisions you didn’t even make.

 

“You already know life isn’t fair. But you need not make it dark, or unpleasant as well. In Hufflepuff, you would find true friendship, something real that you have been missing. You would appreciate the honest hard work, enjoy the fellowship of others and I promise you, whatever you achieve, it will be greater in the company of your friends and fellows than it will be alone.”

 

A rebellious part of Harry idly wondered what sort of reaction he might get by becoming the first Dark Lord to rise from Hufflepuff.

 

The Hat snickered, or well, it was a mental sort of laughter, an odd sensation. 

 

"This day is a great fork in your destiny. Don't be so sure that there will be other choices beyond this one. There is no road-sign set, to mark the place of your last chance to turn back. If you refuse one chance will you not refuse others? It may be that your fate is already sealed, even by deciding this one thing."

 

Harry paused, to seriously consider this. This situation, or something very much like it, occurred often in his Fantasy and Science Fiction novels. The Hero, or the Villain, would meet an Oracle, a purveyor of wisdom, and receive a warning about their choices, the paths which might lead to darkness. And in thinking of this, he knew that of those who ignored the Oracle, or chose wrongly anyway, few, very few, ended up with a happy ending. If was honest with himself, about wanting to achieve his potential, and also wanting to do good, then shouldn’t he listen here, heed the Hat’s warning? It was an unpleasant thought, one his mind tried to shy away from.

 

Harry sighed mentally, he felt defeated. Fine, let’s get this over with. Just sort me where I belong...

 

“Ha! You entirely forgot to demand the secrets of the lost magic that created me. And they were such wonderful, important secrets, too”

You sly little BASTARD -

“You deserved it.”

 

Before Harry could demand them, or complain further, the silence of the Hall was broken by a single word.

"HUFFLEPUFF!"

Some students screamed, the pent-up tension was so great. People startled hard enough to fall off their benches. Hagrid clapped first, while McGonagall merely staggered at the podium, and Snape dropped the remains of his heavy silver goblet directly onto his groin.

Harry sat there frozen, still in shock from the conversation. He wished he could have chosen to be in Ravenclaw, to remain in the same house as Hermione. Even as he thought it, his eyes found her at the Ravenclaw table. Hermione was beaming up at him, clapping with a few of the other Ravenclaw first-year students. Maybe this would turn out okay.
