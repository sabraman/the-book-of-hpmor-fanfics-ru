Chapter 1

Extrapolating from the parallels between Harry's "General Chaos" and Tom Riddle's "Lord Voldemort" personas… I made a thing:

Men in skull masks and ill-fitted black robes were staring at Tom Morfin Riddle, who was disguised (thanks to an intentionally failed Polyjuice in which he’d purposefully slipped a snake scale) as a terrifying red-eyed snake-man, who had introduced himself to them as Lord Voldemort the day before. Those second-rate minions knew what to call themselves and who they were to answer to, they were wearing their uniforms, and what little clear goals Voldemort had were deeply ingrained in their feeble brains. Tonight was the night. Tonight, he would lead the first raid of his mockup reign of terror. But Riddle felt that a final motivational speech could not hurt, and that he would need experience with those as well; so he began:

“My Death Eaters, I am not going to lie to you, our situation today is very grim. The holders of the Line of Merlin have never lost a single battle. The truth is, most of you are probably going to die. And the survivors will envy the dead. But we have to win this. We have to win this so that someday, our children can enjoy the sparkles of a wand again. Everything is at stake here. Literally everything. If we lose, magic itself will vanish like a Horcrux dipped in Basilisk venom. And I now realize that most of you don't know what a Horcrux is. Well, take it from me… this is bad. But if we have to go down, let's go down fighting, like the heroes we are, so that as the darkness closes in, we can think to ourselves, at least we had fun. Are you afraid to die? I know I am.”

(there was a hint of amusement in Tom Riddle’s hissing voice, which the Death Eaters were much too drunk to notice.)

“I can feel those cold shivers of fear, as surely as if someone had cast a Cooling Charm on my robes. But I know... that History is watching us. It was watching us when you changed into your uniforms. It was probably taking pictures. And history, my troops, is written by the victors. If we win this, we can write our own history. A history in which Hogwarts was founded by Salazar Slytherin alone. We can make everyone study that history, even though it isn't true, and if they don't answer the right way on our tests... we’ll Crucio them out of school. Isn't that worth dying for? No, don't answer that. Some things are better left unknown. None of you kneww why you were here. None of you knew that they would be fighting. You found yourself with me in this alley, suddenly knowing only that there was no way to get your names and your honor back except victory. The blood traitors are like us, you know. They don't want to die. They're fighting to protect each other and the mudbloods, who are the only friends they have left. They may even be fighting to save the world. But you have a better reason to fight than they do. You will fight because you like it. You fight to amuse me, an eldritch monstrosity from beyond Space and Time. You will fight because you are Death Eaters. Soon the war will begin, so let me say now, because I won't get a chance later, that it was an honor to be your commander, however briefly. Thank you, thank you all. And remember, your goal isn't just to cut down the enemy, it's to make them afraid.”

Tom Riddle was an expert at reading his fellow men’s emotions in their behaviour, and as soon as he had finished his speech, he’d begun noticing that confusion spread rapidly as the men in the skull masks looked at each other. None of them were too sure they’d understood half of what their Master had said, and that was worrying them. Was this mysterious wizard not the right leader for their cause, after all?

The snake-man groaned before obliviating the lot of them, stripping them of their Death Eater robes, and going home, determined to try again in the morrow.
