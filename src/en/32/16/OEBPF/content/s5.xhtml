Chapter 5: Transfiguration

As the gargoyles settled back into stony immobility, Minerva turned to Harry.

"We shall discuss this further at a later time, Mr Potter. Right now, you and I have a Transfiguration class to attend."

She turned. "Filius, can we count on your assistance?"

The tiny man nodded. "Of course, Minerva."

He turned and walked away.

Harry and Professor McGonagall hurried towards the Transfiguration classroom, Fawkes and Bentham retaining their positions on Harry's shoulders. Professor McGonagall spoke first.

"Will you oppose the Headmaster directly, Mr Potter?"

"That's certainly a possibility now, Professor. I honestly wish I didn't have to, but I may not have a choice. The Headmaster is willing to let the good guys lose a lot more than I can tolerate, and in this instance he doesn't seem to be aware of the dangers to begin with. Heroic responsibility, you understand – children will suffer and die if I don't do something, and it would be my fault."

"I understand. Be careful, Mr Potter, and good luck."

"Thank you, Professor."

They walked the rest of the way in thoughtful silence.

They entered the room to find it deathly silent. Harry quietly dropped into a spare seat at the back of the room, while Professor McGonagall strode to the front. Unnoticed by Harry, Bentham had faded to the translucent shimmer of an alcohol flame. Fawkes retained his accustomed plumage, but was maintaining something like a Notice-Me-Not charm. Neither bird was noticed by any of the students who turned to gawk briefly at Harry before their attention was captured by Professor McGonagall.

"Today, children," began the calm professional voice of the Transfiguration Professor, just as though nothing out of the ordinary had happened, "we shall learn how much effort it takes to sustain a Transfiguration, and why, at your age, you should not even try. The original Form is not gone, only suppressed; and to maintain that suppression - "

"Excuse me," said Padma Patil. Her voice was shaking and she was visibly trembling, but still she asked the question. "Excuse me, Professor, but what happened with Miss Granger?"

Professor McGonagall paused, then seemed to slump very slightly as she turned to face Padma. "Oh, very well. I suppose this is a matter of public record by now, and I quite see why you are concerned. The story is hardly mine to tell, however - Mr Potter, if you would?"

Harry, the peace of the phoenix upon him, stood up calmly and strode to the front of the classroom. A gasp went around the room as the two phoenixes shimmered into view on his shoulders, but Harry ignored it; he merely bowed slightly to Professor McGonagall. "Thank you, Professor - I appreciate the consideration."

He turned to Padma and the rest of the class.

"I shall begin, Miss Patil, by stating once more that Miss Granger is entirely innocent - you no doubt realise this already, but I must repeat it once more for the benefit of the others in this room. Miss Granger committed one morally questionable act, and one only; when she woke up believing that she had probably killed Mr Malfoy, she did not immediately turn herself in. The entire affair was an elaborate frame job, carried out by a skilled adult. Oh, and Mr Malfoy is innocent also; his only morally questionable act was to arrange a midnight duel with Miss Granger. Remember this, all of you."

Harry stood straight, face deadly serious.

"Now, as for what actually happened today. You all deserve to know how the Wizengamot actually works, and what sort of a world we actually live in. You also deserve to know how and why it is now changing."

He glanced around the room at his classmates, all of whom were staring intently at him.

"Lord Malfoy was furious, understandably. He declared a Life Debt, and, less understandably, with a little prompting from Lord Jugson he called for Miss Granger to be sent to Azkaban.

The young faces before Harry now wore near-identical looks of slack-jawed horror.

"The Wizengamot agreed. To be clear: earlier today, the Wizengamot voted to send Hermione Granger to Azkaban."

Many of the students cast questioning looks towards Professor McGonagall, but she merely nodded grimly. Harry continued.

"I declared Lord Malfoy's life debt to me for killing You-Know-Who, and offered it up to keep Miss Granger out of Azkaban. Lord Malfoy accepted, but demanded a hundred thousand Galleons as well."

All the other students gasped at once; wealthy families or not, that was an obscene penalty. Harry went on.

"I tried to accept, but Professor Dumbledore overruled me - apparently he's my guardian. To be clear: the Headmaster of Hogwarts acted to send Hermione Granger to Azkaban. I won't call him a bad person, but you should all understand what he's willing in service of what he believes is right. I warned him that I wouldn't let Hermione Granger go to Azkaban, but he didn't believe me."

Harry paused, and gestured towards Bentham.

"Bentham here came to me as I stood there, and he took me to Azkaban before Hermione could be taken there. I used my Patronus - yes, I do have one, but it's weird - I used my Patronus to destroy every Dementor in Azkaban."

The room was thrown into uproar, which lasted approximately two seconds before Professor McGonagall cast a wide-angle silencing charm. She dispelled it a moment later, but the silence held. Harry continued.

"As I was saying, I destroyed every Dementor in Azkaban before returning to the Hall of the Wizengamot. I then resolved my differences with Lord Malfoy and most of the Wizengamot; Hermione won't be going to Azkaban, and I'm not going into debt to House Malfoy. I also took up my family title - technically I'm Lord Potter now, though I will still answer to 'Harry'."

He grinned cheekily, savouring the looks of bewilderment and disbelief on his classmates' faces.

"And to answer your question more directly, Miss Patil: Miss Granger is currently asleep in the infirmary, suffering nothing worse than emotional exhaustion."

Padma sagged with relief, and Terry Boot spoke up.

"So who is this 'skilled adult' who framed Hermione?"

Harry spread his hands.

"I don't know, Mr Boot. I have my suspicions, but I can't actually prove anything."

Terry looked thoughtful.

"So has Professor Quirrell been arrested yet?"

Harry smiled faintly.

"I believe that Professor Quirrell was in fact being held for questioning, but he's probably been released already. As far as the Ministry is concerned there was no adult perpetrator - only a twelve-year-old girl guilty of attempted murder. And as for your implied accusation of Professor Quirrell for the adult crime which we know to have been committed, I can neither confirm nor deny anything about my private suspicions in relation to any individual."

Kevin Entwhistle spoke. "Is that Dumbledore's phoenix on your other shoulder?"

Harry nodded. "Fawkes, yes."

"Is he your phoenix now? Can that even happen?"

"I suppose it is possible, but no. Fawkes will still go back to Dumbledore."

"And is the other one - Bentham? - your phoenix now? Like, will he stay with you?"

Harry nodded again. "I believe so."

Michael Boot spoke up.

"General, you've deliberately told us bad things about the Headmaster - you even said it was because you wanted us to know. Are you planning to lead students in open defiance of the Headmaster?"

Professor McGonagall flinched at that, although fortunately no one was looking at her. Harry, at whom everyone was now staring, did not flinch. Instead he paused for a moment before casting a faintly apologetic glance at Professor McGonagall, who merely looked resigned. He then turned back to Terry, face now showing something of his anger.

"That, Mr Boot, is an excellent question."

Harry then told the story of his meeting with Professors McGonagall and Flitwick, though he did omit the more personal discussion between himself and Minerva. He told the story of their meeting with the Headmaster, omitting nothing except the details of the specific safety precautions suggested.

"And so you see, Mr Boot, that the Headmaster is so worried about me that he is willing to compromise the safety and security of Hogwarts and its students rather than listen to my suggestions. I do not do this lightly, of course, but I am indeed planning to lead students in at least applying pressure to the Headmaster to change his mind. I do respect the institution of Headmaster, and I do respect Professor Dumbledore himself, but I value your safety – our safety – much more highly than any of that."

Harry looked around at the shocked faces, but there were no more questions. He retook his seat at the back of the room, and Professor McGonagall began her class.
