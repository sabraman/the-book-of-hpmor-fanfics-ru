Chapter 6: Welcoming Feast

Once we'd learned what houses we'd be living in, and a few of the Wizard-born students were beginning to wonder aloud whether we would now be able to go eat, Professor Sprout called out for everybody to quiet down, and explained that we would now enter the Great Hall where we would sit at our House tables, but that before the food arrived we would get to meet the Sorting Hat after all. The new Sorting Ceremony would not sort us into houses, but into likely future careers. This Career Sorting would foster more personalized mentoring by Hogwarts faculty, she explained.

Ginny has her doubts about the usefulness of this new type of Sorting, given that we all still take the same core classes and still get to choose our electives. She reckons that the real purpose of the new Sorting Ceremony is so that the Sorting Hat doesn't go insane with boredom. She actually mentioned this hypothesis at one of the early meetings of Chaos Club, which launched a lively debate. Harry Potter-Evans-Verres was uncharacteristically silent for that discussion, and mostly sat there looking uncomfortable while the conversation ran its course. I never found out why he reacted that way, but I guess it's probably related to a rumour I heard that at some point after the Welcoming Feast, older students were given the option of Career Sorting if they wanted, but that the Hat had refused point-blank to be placed onto Harry's head.

Whether Ginny's idea is right or not, during our Career Sorting, the Sorting Hat announced that Ginny was well suited to work in the Ministry, that Luna should be prepared for training as a Healer, and that I was likely to go on to a career in journalism. I wonder whether part of Ginny's scepticism is rooted in annoyance at the suggestion that she might follow in her father's footsteps. For other students, the Sorting Hat suggested careers like Auror (for any Muggles reading this, that's the name for the police in Magical Britain), magical artefact development, or banking. There were gasps of shock all around the room at the first mention of banking, and I heard a couple of students at the Slytherin table mutter things about how the goblins wouldn't be happy, or quietly grumbling that we were headed for another goblin rebellion.

Once we'd all settled ourselves again, the Headmistress stood up from the teachers' table, and spoke in her loud strong voice, "Welcome to a new year at Hogwarts!" and then I heard a catch in her voice, almost as though she were about to cry, as she continued, "Before we begin our banquet, I would like to say a few words." She paused, then took a deep breath before going on, her voice strengthening once more as she spoke, "No, I am no Albus Dumbledore, and there is no replacing him. He was not always the wisest of Headmasters, but it is nevertheless a great honour to have had the privilege of calling him my colleague. However," and here the tall witch smiled slightly, "in his honour, let me repeat for you a few words that he would have liked for you to hear: Nitwit! Blubber! Oddment! Tweak! Thank you." And so saying, she sat down again to a rather sedated round of applause from the school.

At this moment, the Hogwarts House Elves, who had been lined up in the back of the Hall, clapped their hands in unison, and dishes of hot food floated out of a hidden doorway, landing gently on the five tables. The food was quite good, though nothing like home. I was ravenous, which I guess makes sense. It had been a long day: train ride, encounter with a giant, boat ride, finding out where I'd be living for the next year, and Career Sorting. Anyhow, I ate quickly, focused on little else, and was barely finished when I slowly realized that the Great Hall had fallen into a sort of hush of anticipation. All eyes were on the Faculty Table, and when I followed the collective gaze, I saw that Headmistress McGonnagall was standing up again. Looking back, I suppose that most of the student body had been waiting rather impatiently for the more substantial speech traditionally given by the Headmistress or Headmaster at the end of the Welcoming Feast.

So many changes this year, Luna had said. Of course the students were waiting. Most the speech that followed focused school policy, which evidently was not very different from previous years, then something about Quidditch tryouts with reference to the elimination of one of the positions. There was a general murmur of resigned discontent at that. Finally, she went on to discuss the changes in course offerings and to introduce new faculty. Based on the responses of my classmates, this was the really interesting part.

Professor Bones would be teaching Battle Tactics and Strategy, a more general form of the old Hogwarts class called Defense Against the Dark Arts, and Professor Lupin, the new Head of Gryffindor House, would be teaching a new upper level elective called Magical Anthropology. The goal of the class on Magical Anthropology was to promote better relations among Wizards, Muggles, and what Professor McGonnagall referred to as, "Non-Human Sentients." Professor Lupin, she explained, was a werewolf, and therefore uniquely qualified to instruct us on the cultural differences among these groups. I was a little startled to learn that a werewolf would be teaching, but Professor Lupin didn't look nearly as intimidating as Mr. Hagrid, and Professor McGonnagall was so manner-of-fact about it that I realized I wasn't particularly upset. The other students seemed to accept this happily enough too, though Ginny whispered, "Before Fixus Everythingus, that announcement would have caused rioting," and then very seriously added, "I'm not exaggerating."

Then the Headmistress went on to announce that classes in Muggle Science had been added for each year. Given the textbook lists, this announcement had been long-expected by most students, and was met by a quiet murmur of acknowledgement. The murmur became angry when she went on to say that the new courses would be replacing History of Magic. I briefly assumed that History of Magic had been a particularly popular course, but Ginny explained in a low voice, "Most of them count on History of Magic as a chance to catch up on sleep."

Finally McGonnagall introduced the new Professor of Muggle Science, Professor Evans-Verres. McGonnagall explained that some evidence had come to light that Muggle Science had much to offer her own field of Transfiguration, and that she had high hopes that other magical subjects would benefit as well. At Professor Evans-Verres' insistence, Arithmancy would now be a required subject as well, replacing Astronomy, which would now be available as an upper level elective. This arrangement would permit Professor Sinistra the time she would require to bring herself up to speed on the past two hundred years of Muggle Astronomy, as well as to expand her independent research.

"For my own part," the Headmistress continued, "I shall be collaborating directly with Professor Evans-Verres on the development of a new research program on the use of Muggle Science to analyse Transfiguration and the applications of Muggle Science to Transfiguration. I am looking forward to those collaboration as what I am certain shall be a very fruitful endeavour, and I know that all of you will do your best to make Professor Evans-Verres and his wife Petunia Verres-Evans welcome at this school."

At that moment, I heard some slight snickering on the other side of the hall and saw Fred and George whispering to each other at the Gryffindor table.

McGonnagall raised a severe eyebrow, and to my surprise the Weasley twins went instantly silent and looked up, very seriously. The Headmistress spoke then in a low and dangerous voice, "As you may have inferred, my extensive responsibilities will afford me little time for nonsense, and I trust that we shall have none. Have no fear, however, that my duties as Headmistress will be neglected. Should my teaching and research duties every collide with my responsibilities as Headmistress, Deputy Headmaster Flitwick shall be standing by."

Professor Flitwick gave a brief sombre nod of confirmation, and then McGonnagall concluded her speech in lighter tones, welcoming us once more to Hogwarts and wishing us all a restful night.
